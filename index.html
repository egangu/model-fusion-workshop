Overview

Open model ecosystems now contain large numbers of pretrained checkpoints, fine-tuned variants, adapters, domain-specific models, and model updates. FuseFM focuses on how these distributed contributions can be reused, composed, distilled, updated, evaluated, and governed as reliable foundation model systems.

The workshop is designed as a focused meeting point for researchers working on model merging, model soups, task vectors, adapter and LoRA fusion, model stitching, representation alignment, modular composition, knowledge distillation, continual updates, model editing, systems support, provenance, licensing, and evaluation.

Topics of Interest

Model fusion methodsMerging, soups, task vectors, weight-space arithmetic, adapter and LoRA fusion.
Distillation and transferKnowledge distillation, on-policy distillation, modular transfer, model editing, and continual updates.
ArchitecturesLLMs, multimodal models, diffusion models, policy models, MoE models, and scientific foundation models.
EvaluationCapability retention, interference, forgetting, calibration, robustness, safety, and diagnostic benchmarks.
SystemsModel registries, compatibility tests, reproducible fusion pipelines, serving, and versioning.
GovernanceProvenance, licensing metadata, reporting standards, responsible reuse, and community norms.

Workshop Format

FuseFM will combine invited talks, contributed spotlights, posters, demos, a panel, and structured breakouts. The goal is not only to present finished results, but also to identify shared terminology, evaluation gaps, and reporting standards for collaborative foundation model development.
