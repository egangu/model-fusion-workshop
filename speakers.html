Speakers

Fei Wu Zhejiang University Speaker

Model Fusion for Collaborative Foundation Model Development

This talk will define the workshop problem and connect model fusion to collaborative foundation-model development.

Jingrui He University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign Speaker

Reliable Evaluation of Fused Foundation Models

Model fusion can improve reuse and adaptation, but it can also introduce interference, forgetting, calibration shifts, and hidden failure modes. This talk will discuss evaluation protocols for fused models, including stress tests for robustness, reliability, fairness, and capability retention across tasks and domains.

Wei Wang University of California, Los Angeles Speaker

Data and Systems Infrastructure for Reproducible Model Fusion

Collaborative model fusion depends on more than algorithms: it needs scalable data pipelines, metadata, provenance, compatibility checks, and reproducible workflows. This talk will examine systems and data-management principles for making fused foundation model development easier to audit, reproduce, and maintain.

Simon Shaolei Du University of Washington Speaker

Optimization and Generalization Principles for Model Composition

Why do some models, updates, or adapters compose cleanly while others interfere? This talk will outline theoretical perspectives on optimization landscapes, representation alignment, task vectors, and generalization in model composition, with an emphasis on open problems that can guide empirical model fusion research.

Xia Hu Shanghai AI Laboratory Speaker

Trustworthy Model Fusion: Safety, Robustness, and Governance

Fusing models or updates can transfer useful capabilities, but it may also transfer unsafe behavior, privacy risks, backdoors, or licensing ambiguity. This talk will focus on trustworthiness questions for fused foundation models, including safety evaluation, provenance, governance, and responsible reuse in open model ecosystems.

Jianbo Xuan Tencent Hunyuan Speaker

Model Reuse and Fusion in Industrial Foundation Model Development

Industrial foundation model development often requires continuous updates, domain adaptation, model reuse, and deployment under tight reliability and efficiency constraints. This talk will discuss practical lessons from large-scale foundation model systems and identify where model fusion can support safer, faster, and more maintainable deployment pipelines.
