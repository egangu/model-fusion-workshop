Scope

FuseFM welcomes work on algorithms, evaluation, systems, and governance for model fusion. Submissions may study new methods, empirical analyses, benchmark design, systems infrastructure, responsible model reuse, negative results, or critical position statements.

Topics

  • Model merging, model soups, task vectors, weight-space arithmetic, and model stitching.
  • Adapter fusion, LoRA / PEFT composition, modular transfer, and representation alignment.
  • Knowledge distillation, on-policy distillation, model editing, and continual model updates.
  • Fusion across LLMs, multimodal models, diffusion models, policy models, MoE models, and scientific foundation models.
  • Evaluation of capability retention, interference, forgetting, calibration, robustness, safety, and backdoors.
  • Model registries, compatibility tests, versioning, licensing metadata, provenance, and reproducible pipelines.

Reviewing

Submissions will be reviewed through OpenReview. Accepted papers may be linked from OpenReview or this website at the authors' option; they will not appear in formal proceedings.

Reviewers will assess topical fit, technical soundness, novelty, clarity, reproducibility or artifact value, potential to stimulate workshop discussion, and responsible model reuse, including provenance, licensing, safety, and evaluation awareness where relevant. Reviewers may not use LLMs or automated agents to assist with reviews.

Organizers, their PhD students and postdocs, and people with personal conflicts of interest with the organizers will not submit papers to this workshop. Organizers will not assess submissions from their own institutions or other conflicted authors. Review assignments and acceptance decisions will follow NeurIPS conflict-of-interest rules.

Work that is already finalized, formally published, or accepted to the NeurIPS 2026 main conference should not be submitted. The workshop is intended for discussion, work in progress, new perspectives, benchmarks, systems, demos, and non-archival feedback.
